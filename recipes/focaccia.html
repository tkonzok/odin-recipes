Focaccia

Description

Focaccia is a flat bread similar to pizza dough that can be either sweet or savory. 1/2 to 1 inch thick, with a light crust and an surface full of indentations that hold oil.

Ingredients

  • 512 g flour
  • 8 g yeast
  • 10 g salt
  • 455 g water
  • Olive oil
  • Rosemary

Steps

  1. Gather all ingredients.
  2. Whisk together the flour, salt, and yeast.
  3. Add the water.
  4. Use a spatula to stir the two together.
  5. Slick the surface of the dough with olive oil; then cover the bowl. Stick the bowl in the fridge immediately; leave it there to rise for 12 to 18 hours or longer.
  6. Deflate the dough and transfer to a prepared pan. Don’t touch the dough again for 2 to 4 hours depending on your environment.
  7. After two to four hours it’s time to dimple it! You can use simply olive oil and salt. If you are using rosemary, sprinkle it over the dough. Then pour two tablespoons of olive oil over the dough, and using your fingers, press straight down to create deep dimples. Sprinkle with flaky sea salt.
  8. Transfer to oven immediately and bake at 220ºC for 25 minutes or until golden all around. Remove focaccia from pans and place on cooling racks.
